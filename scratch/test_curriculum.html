Pulmonary Edema Pathophysiology

The most common cause of pulmonary edema is heart failure. When the heart is not able to pump efficiently, blood can back up into the veins that take blood through the lungs.

Key Concepts

  • Increased hydrostatic pressure in pulmonary capillaries.
  • Fluid leakage into the interstitial space.
  • Impairs oxygen diffusion across the alveolar-capillary membrane.

Question: What is the primary mechanism of cardiogenic pulmonary edema?

  • A. Increased capillary hydrostatic pressure
  • B. Decreased plasma oncotic pressure
  • C. Increased capillary permeability
  • D. Lymphatic obstruction

Correct Answer: A

Explanation: Increased left atrial pressure leads to back-pressure in the pulmonary veins and capillaries, forcing fluid into the alveoli.
