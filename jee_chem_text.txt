Bohr's model of the atom was a major step in understanding atomic structure. It proposed that electrons move in fixed, circular orbits around the nucleus, similar to planets around the sun. Each orbit has a specific energy level, and electrons can only move between these levels by absorbing or emitting a photon of energy. To find the electronic configuration, we use the Aufbau principle, which states that electrons fill the lowest energy subshells first, following the order 1s, 2s, 2p, and so on.
