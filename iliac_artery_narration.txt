The internal iliac artery is the primary vascular supplying the pelvic organs and reproductive structures. It bifurcates from the common iliac artery, dividing into anterior and posterior divisions. Understanding these branches is essential for any pelvic surgical procedure.

Let's look at our options to find the one that does NOT belong.
First, the Superior Vesical artery. This is a key branch of the anterior division, supplying the upper bladder.
Next, the Uterine artery. In females, this is a major branch of the internal iliac, critical for uterine blood supply.
Then, the Internal Pudendal artery. This is another fundamental branch that exits the pelvis to supply the external genitalia.
Finally, let's examine the Ovarian artery. Unlike the others, the ovarian artery arises directly from the abdominal aorta, passing into the pelvis from above.

Therefore, we have our answer. The ovarian artery is NOT a branch of the internal iliac artery. The correct answer is A.
