In Physics, a Velocity-Time graph is a powerful tool to understand motion. For uniform acceleration, the graph is a straight line with a constant slope. The slope of this line represents the acceleration of the object. More importantly, the area under the velocity-time graph gives us the total displacement. If we have a constant acceleration 'a', the area forms a trapezoid, leading us directly to the kinematic equation: s equals u t plus half a t squared.
