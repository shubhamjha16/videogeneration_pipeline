Subject-verb agreement is a frequent area of testing in English competitive exams. The basic rule is that a singular subject needs a singular verb, while a plural subject needs a plural verb. However, things get tricky with indefinite pronouns like 'everyone' or 'each', which are always singular. For example, we say 'everyone is here', not 'everyone are here'. Another common trap is the 'one of' structure; in the phrase 'one of the students', the subject is 'one', so it takes a singular verb, like 'one of the students is missing'.
