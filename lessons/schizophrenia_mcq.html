Concept Explanation

Schizophrenia is a chronic psychiatric disorder characterized by disturbances in thought, perception, and behavior. Antipsychotic medications, such as risperidone, are used for both the acute and maintenance (continuation) phases of treatment to prevent relapse. The duration of maintenance therapy after the first episode of schizophrenia depends on factors such as the risk of relapse, side effects, patient’s age, and whether it’s a first or recurrent episode. Guidelines exist to help clinicians decide how long to continue antipsychotic medication after symptom remission.

Guidelines for Treatment Duration

For a first episode of schizophrenia, most guidelines recommend maintenance antipsychotic therapy for at least 1–2 years after achieving remission. This is because the risk of relapse is highest in the first two years. If the patient remains stable and side effects are manageable, treatment can sometimes be continued longer, especially if risk factors for relapse are present. Shorter durations (e.g., 6 months) are generally not recommended except under specific circumstances, and longer durations (e.g., 5 years or indefinite) are reserved for individuals with multiple episodes or persistent risk factors.

Analysis of Options

A. 5 years
This duration is generally recommended for individuals with multiple episodes, high risk of relapse, or persistent symptoms. Since the patient has only a first episode and no family history, this is longer than usually necessary for initial maintenance.

B. 6 months
Six months is shorter than what most clinical guidelines recommend. Relapse risk remains high within this period post-remission, so stopping medication at 6 months is not advised after the first episode.

C. 2 years
Two years of continued antipsychotic therapy after achieving remission is in accordance with current guidelines for a first episode of schizophrenia without additional risk factors. This duration balances relapse prevention and minimizing long-term side effects.

D. 12 months
One year is the minimum some guidelines suggest for maintenance after a first episode, but the consensus generally favors 1 to 2 years. In practice, many clinicians choose the 2-year mark for greater safety, especially in young patients.

Correct Answer

C. 2 years is the most appropriate duration for maintenance therapy with risperidone in this case, as per the majority of clinical guidelines for first-episode schizophrenia.
