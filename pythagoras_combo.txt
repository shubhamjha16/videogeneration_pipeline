Hello student! Today we explore one of the most famous rules in geometry: The Pythagorean Theorem.
This theorem allows us to find the missing side of a right-angled triangle if we know the other two sides. Let's see how it works.

---
[M]
Consider a right-angled triangle with sides a, b, and the longest side c, called the hypotenuse.
The theorem states that a squared plus b squared equals c squared.
For example, if a is 3 and b is 4.
Then 3 squared is 9, and 4 squared is 16.
Summing them gives 25, which is exactly 5 squared.
So our hypotenuse c is 5!
This 3, 4, 5 combination is known as a Pythagorean Triple.
