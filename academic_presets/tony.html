Concept Explanation

The internal iliac artery is a major artery that supplies blood to the pelvic region, gluteal region, and the reproductive organs. It branches off from the common iliac artery and then divides into anterior and posterior divisions, each giving off several branches. These branches supply various pelvic and perineal structures.

Understanding its branches is crucial for professionals dealing with pelvic surgeries or obstetric and gynecological procedures, as well as understanding the vascular supply of the pelvis for diagnostic and treatment purposes.

Option Analysis

  • A. Ovarian artery
    The ovarian artery is not a branch of the internal iliac artery. It arises directly from the abdominal aorta and then descends into the pelvis to supply the ovaries.
  • B. Superior vesical artery
    The superior vesical artery is a branch of the internal iliac artery, typically arising from the anterior division. It supplies the upper part of the bladder and, in males, may supply part of the ductus deferens.
  • C. Uterine artery
    The uterine artery is also a branch of the anterior division of the internal iliac artery. It supplies blood to the uterus and other structures in the female pelvis.
  • D. Internal pudendal artery
    The internal pudendal artery is another important branch of the anterior division of the internal iliac artery. It leaves the pelvis through the greater sciatic foramen and supplies the perineum and external genitalia.

Conclusion

The correct answer is: A. Ovarian artery
