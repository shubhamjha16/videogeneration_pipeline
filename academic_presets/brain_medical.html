Which part of the brain is responsible for regulating heart rate, breathing, and blood pressure?

A. Cerebellum
B. Medulla Oblongata
C. Cerebral Cortex
D. Thalamus

Concept Explanation

The Medulla Oblongata is the lowermost part of the brainstem, connecting the brain to the spinal cord. It contains vital autonomic centers that control involuntary life-sustaining functions including heart rate, respiratory rate, blood pressure regulation, and reflexes like swallowing, coughing, and vomiting.

The brainstem consists of three parts: midbrain (mesencephalon), pons, and medulla oblongata. The medulla is the most caudal and is directly continuous with the spinal cord at the foramen magnum.

Option Analysis

A. Cerebellum — Wrong. The cerebellum coordinates voluntary movement, balance, and fine motor control. It does NOT control autonomic functions like heart rate or breathing.
B. Medulla Oblongata — CORRECT. Contains the cardiac center (controls heart rate), vasomotor center (blood pressure), and respiratory center (breathing rhythm). These are critical survival centers.
C. Cerebral Cortex — Wrong. The cerebral cortex handles higher cognitive functions — thinking, memory, language, voluntary movement. Not autonomic control.
D. Thalamus — Wrong. The thalamus is a relay station for sensory and motor signals to the cerebral cortex. It does not directly regulate heart rate or breathing.

Final Answer

B. Medulla Oblongata

The medulla oblongata houses the cardiac, vasomotor, and respiratory centers — making it the brain's life-support control room.

Key Points to Remember

  • Medulla = autonomic vital centers (heart, lungs, BP)
  • Cerebellum = balance and coordination
  • Thalamus = sensory relay station
  • Cerebral Cortex = higher cognition and voluntary movement
  • Damage to medulla = immediately life-threatening
