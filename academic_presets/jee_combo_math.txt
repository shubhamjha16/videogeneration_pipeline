[P]
In a triangle ABC, let the sides opposite to angles A, B, and C be denoted by a, b, and c respectively. The Law of Cosines states that:
a² = b² + c² - 2bc cos(A)
b² = a² + c² - 2ac cos(B)
c² = a² + b² - 2ab cos(C)
For this particular problem, we are given that angle B is 45 degrees.
---
[M]
Since angle B = 45 degrees, we have b² = a² + c² - 2ac cos(45°).
Substituting cos(45°) = 1/sqrt(2), we get b² = a² + c² - sqrt(2)ac.
The goal is to find the value of a⁴ + b⁴ + c⁴ in terms of a, b, and c.
By squaring both sides of b² + sqrt(2)ac = a² + c², and simplifying, we can find the correct relationship.
The result shows that Option C, which is 2b²(a² + c²), is the correct answer.
