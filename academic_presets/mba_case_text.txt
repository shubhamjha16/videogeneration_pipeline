In today's competitive landscape, many startups face the paradox of low pricing but high churn. Let's analyze a case where slow delivery and poor support are driving customers away despite cost advantages.
From an MBA perspective, we must determine what to prioritize first. While both are critical, the core product promise of a startup is its value chain reliability.
Slow delivery is a fundamental operational failure. It affects the primary customer experience and creates a backlog of tickets for customer support.
Therefore, the company should prioritize delivery speed first. By fixing operations, you reduce the volume of support queries, effectively solving two problems at once.
Improving support without fixing delivery is simply managing dissatisfaction. Fixing delivery builds trust and fulfills the primary promise of the brand.
In conclusion, operational excellence must precede service recovery. Solve the root cause, and the customer lifetime value will eventually recover.
