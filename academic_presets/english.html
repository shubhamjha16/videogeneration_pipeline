Concept Explanation: Active vs Passive Voice

Voice shows whether the subject of a sentence performs the action (active voice) or receives the action (passive voice).

Active Voice: The subject performs the action.
Example: "Some people were helping the wounded woman."
(Subject: Some people, Object: The wounded woman)

Passive Voice: The original object becomes the subject.
Example: "The wounded woman was being helped by some people."

Tense Shift (Past Continuous):
Active: being + helping → Passive: was being helped.

Option Analysis

  • A. "The wounded woman is being helped by some people."
    Incorrect. Changes the tense from past continuous ("were helping") to present continuous ("is being helped").
  • B. "The wounded woman was being helped by some people."
    Correct. Preserves both the original meaning and the past continuous tense correctly in passive form.
  • C. "The wounded is helped by some people."
    Incorrect. Changes tense to simple present and is grammatically awkward.
  • D. "Two wounded woman was helped by some people."
    Incorrect. Changes the number (two women) and tense (simple past).

Correct Answer

B. "The wounded woman was being helped by some people."
