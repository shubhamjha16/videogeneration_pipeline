Concept Explanation: Basics of Probability

Probability is the branch of mathematics that measures the likelihood of an event occurring. It is expressed as a number between 0 and 1, where 0 indicates impossibility and 1 indicates certainty. The basic formula is: P(A) = Number of favorable outcomes / Total number of possible outcomes. For example, when tossing a fair coin, the probability of getting 'Heads' is 1/2 because there is 1 favorable outcome (Heads) out of 2 possible outcomes (Heads and Tails).

Numerical Problem: Sum of Two Dice

When rolling two fair six-sided dice, what is the probability that the sum of the two numbers is exactly 7?

Step-by-Step Solution:
1. First, find the total number of possible outcomes. Each die has 6 sides, so 6 x 6 = 36 total outcomes.
2. Second, list the favorable outcomes that sum to 7: (1,6), (2,5), (3,4), (4,3), (5,2), (6,1).
3. Next, count these outcomes. There are 6 favorable outcomes.
4. Finally, apply the formula: P(Sum=7) = 6 / 36, which simplifies to 1/6.
